Our Story

As we live in a society that heavily relies on computer technology, the strategy of advertising has also shifted towards more usage of computer technology which advertisers have a technique of spreading information faster and in bigger quantities than ever before.

It has also allowed ease of sharing awareness of your product as using the internet has enabled to connect with everyone all around the world.

Our goal is to analyze & explain how different advertisements manipulate emotions/sentiments to achieve advertising effectiveness through viewers' reactions to what they should do and why they should do what is said in the advertisement.

In simpler terms, OAI is aiming to:

  • Analyze what key emotions/sentiments make an advertisement effective
  • Discover specific patterns of viewer reactions to image/video advertisements
  • Create recommendations for your product depending on what you want in an advertisement

Hypotheses

  • The effectiveness of an advertisement can be predicted using topics and sentiments
  • Advertisements cluster together based on their strategies and visual techniques
  • The grouping of advertisements based on their visual techniques change in relation to sentiment type

Data Sources

Image and Video Advertisements

The image and video advertisement data is from Pitt Ad Video and Image Dataset provide information on available topics and sentiments, along with some freeform annotations. The datasets are basically the results of how the annotator interprets the different advertisements. The image dataset contains annotations concerning strategies, visual imagery, and QA action/reason. The video dataset contains annotations concerning the effectiveness, excitingness, funniness, language use, and QA action/reason. Both datasets contain freeform responses or multipe choice responses on what topic and sentiments that the advertisements give off.

Data Preprocessing

The data comes in text and json files. To prepare and organize the data for EDA, we took the data from the text files and manually converted to csv files. Then we used pandas to merge the csv files with the json files into a dataframe, which we converted into a csv to allow for easier access. The two datasets had to be handled differently due to their varying structures in annotations. We also used feature engineering to add sentiment type to help analyze the trends.
